About

I was born on February 15, 2005, at the Mirugi Kariuki Sub Hospital in Nakuru County. My family welcomed me with joy, believing I would become a leader. My father, John Gitau, was a lecturer and director of a youth and women self-help program, while my mother, Virginia Wambui Gitau, was a talented chef and fashion designer. As the youngest and only boy in a family of four, I was nurtured in a supportive environment that fostered my ambitions.

Education

Primary School

I attended Victory Primary School before transferring to Mburu Gishua Primary School due to financial constraints. Despite challenges, I aspired to become an engineer. I became popular for entertaining my peers with rap and gengetone songs, but later focused on my studies and became involved as a youth pastor at PCEA Emmaus Church.

High School

In 2019, I joined Nairobi Road High School, where I was elected as the CU chairman, known as Pastor Jeymo. I organized fundraising events for local children's homes and became a beloved figure among students, completing my high school education in 2022.

University

After high school, I took over my father's land, cultivated crops, and learned skills in welding and CCTV installation. In 2023, I enrolled at the Kenya Institute of Software Engineering and Professional Studies in Thika, pursuing software engineering and robotics. I started a business selling ice pops, earning the nickname "Jeymo the Tycoon."

Leadership Roles

At university, I became vice chairman of the CU and later served as the student president. I founded JEMCO Software & Technologies Solutions Ltd., developing projects that addressed societal needs. My activism led to my election as the Kiambu Student Association President, advocating for university students' rights.

Aspirations

My goal is to create impactful solutions for the challenges facing our country and to foster change in Kenya. I aspire to enter politics and serve in public service roles, using my leadership experience to inspire and uplift others.
